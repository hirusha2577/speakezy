Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How to provide a Multimodal Social Interaction for Speaking Disability Care?

Recent statistics from the World Health Organization (WHO) underscore the global scale of this challenge, revealing that over 360 million people worldwide expe- rience significant hearing loss, with approximately 120,000 individuals born with deaf- mute conditions annually [1]. This demographic, constituting around 5.3/100 of the global population, predominantly comprises adults, highlighting the pressing need for effective communication solutions that bridge the gap between the deaf-mute, mutism community and the wider world [2]. While numerous initiatives and technological so- lutions have been explored to address these communication barriers, the quest for a truly effective system remains ongoing. Traditional approaches have often fallen short in providing a seamless and intuitive means for translating between sign language, miming language, and spoken language, and vice versa [3].


Product Demonstration - Solution

Proposed Solution

Against this backdrop, this research introduces an integrated approach designed to enhance communication for individuals with speech disabilities, specifically within the Sinhala-speaking community. The project incorporates several innovative compo- nents: a Miming Language to Sinhala Text transcription module, a Signing Language to Sinhala Text transcription module, Sinhala Audio Conversion to Miming Language with avatar-based output, and an AI-assisted Sinhala Sign Language Trainer. These tools are engineered to enable the conversion of sign and miming languages into spoken Sinhala and text while also promoting the learning and proficiency of sign language. Utilizing advanced AI technologies and machine learning algorithms, the system seeks to break down communication barriers, creating new avenues for interaction for the deaf-mute and mutism communities. This paper outlines the technology behind each component and discusses their potential to improve communication inclusivity, aiming to make a significant contribution to the dialogue on accessibility and inclusivity.

Research Objectives

AI assisted Sinhala Sign Language Training Platform

An AI-assisted training solution has been developed, allow- ing users to learn and test their Sinhala sign language skills interactively and at their own pace, without needing a physical instructor.

Virtual Meeting Platform option for converting Sinhala Sign Language to Text transcription

The platform offers various learning paths tailored to users' preferences and skill levels, employing two models implemented with CNN and LSTM layers. Due to limited resources for Sinhala sign language, both custom and existing datasets were utilized for training these models, which were then deployed on a Flask server. The project follows agile development practices, ensuring iterative development and responsiveness to feedback.

Virtual Meeting Platform option for converting Sinhala audio to 3D Avatar based lip syncing output with realtime text transcription

For the audio to 3D lip synchronization with the text transcription module, as there are two submodules, one specialized dataset was created to capture the text transcriptions for training purposes. And, for the other module audio dataset was used. For the text transcription module, the dataset was generated through a spe- cially implemented python script where it uses the technology of Wav2Vec2Processor to generate text transcriptions. The script generates the Sinhala text transcriptions when the audio is generated in the same frame. so that it contains efficient processing which served as the dataset for training the DL model to accurately transcribe audio into Sin- hala text. Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed Multimodal Social Interaction for Speaking Disability Care consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. AI assisted Sinhala Sign Language Training Platform
  2. Virtual Meeting Platform option for converting Sinhala Sign Language to Text transcription
  3. Virtual Meeting Platform option for converting Sinhala audio to 3D Avatar based lip syncing output with realtime text transcription
  4. irtual Meeting Platform option for converting Lip syncing to Sinhal text transcription

The research project presents a comprehensive solution aimed at overcoming com- munication barriers for the Sinhala-speaking community with speaking disabilities. It utilizes advanced AI technologies to develop a multifunctional meeting platform that includes Miming Language to Sinhala Text transcription, Signing Language to Sinhala Text transcription, and Sinhala Audio Conversion to Miming Language with avatar- based real-time transcription. Additionally, an AI-assisted Sinhala Sign Language Trainer is integrated into the platform. This platform is designed to be flexible, allowing users to enable accessibility features as needed, while also providing the functionalities of a standard meeting platform. The system's general operations are managed by a de- ployed Node.js server, while tasks requiring trained models and real-time messaging are handled by a Flask server. Extensive data collection and processing were essential to support the training of deep learning models for each component, underpinning the project's goal to enhance digital communication accessibility for individuals with speaking disabilities.
